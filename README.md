**User Manual: SafeChemVRLab**

Welcome to SafeChemVRLab! This virtual reality application provides a hands on, risk free environment to conduct chemistry experiments using the Meta Quest.

**1. Installation and Setup**

•	Because this is a custom Unity build, the application must be installed (sideloaded) onto the Meta Quest using a computer.

•	Connect the Meta Quest to your computer using a USB-C cable.

•	Put on the headset and select Allow USB Debugging when the prompt appears.

•	Open Meta Quest Developer Hub (MQDH) or SideQuest on your computer.

•	Drag and drop the SafeChemVRLab.apk file into the application window.

•	Wait for the "Installation Successful" notification on your computer before unplugging the headset.

**2. Running the VR Application**

•	Once installed, the application is stored in a special developer folder rather than the main store page.

•	Press the Meta button on the right controller to bring up the main menu toolbar.

•	Open the App Library grid of nine squares.

•	Select the Search bar at the top of the library window.

•	Click the dropdown menu next to the search bar usually is labeled "Installed" or "Recent".

•	Scroll to the very bottom of the list and select Unknown Sources.

•	Select SafeChemVRLab to launch the environment.

**3. Navigating the Environment**

•	SafeChemVRLab allows you to move freely around the virtual workspace to approach different lab stations and equipment.

•	Physical Movement: If you have a clear room scale boundary set up, you can physically walk around your room to move within the lab.

•	Continuous Walking: Push the Left Thumbstick forward, backward, left, or right to glide smoothly across the lab floor.

•	Snap Turning: Push the Right Thumbstick left or right to instantly rotate your view, making it easier to look around without physically spinning your body.

•	Audios and Sound Effects: Audios and sound effects should help you understand what you are being asked to perform and what experiments your carrying out.

**4. Interacting with Objects**

•	The laboratory is programmed with realistic physics, requiring you to handle beakers, tools, and chemicals as you would in the real world.

•	Grabbing Items: Point your controllers toward the object until the ray points at an interactive object. Squeeze and hold the Grip Button the button resting under your middle finger to pick it up.

•	Releasing Items: Let go of the Grip Button to drop the object or place it down on a table.

•	Pouring Chemicals: Grab a chemical beaker with one hand, position it over a target beaker, and physically rotate your controller to pour the contents.

•	Operating Equipment: To interact with laboratory tools, such as equipping safety precautions or pressing a quiz button, use the Index Trigger or physically push the button with your virtual index finger.

•	Using Menus and Stations: Point your controller at any floating menu panels or the circular teleportation stations and pull the Index Trigger to click  to attend/make selections.

**5. Instructions to Carry out Experiments**

•	As soon as you enter the application you are met with the voice of our Lab Assistant.

•	Follow his instructions in order to get started.

•	He will instruct you to go to any station of your choice after you teleport there he will follow you there to give you further instructions on how to carry out those experiments.

•	It’s very important that you follow these instructions to have a fun interactive and successful experience.

•	If you fail the lab assistant should provide you with a short feedback on what you did wrong so you can redo the experiment.

•	If you succeed the lab assistant should wish you luck and thank you on being part of this immersive experience.
